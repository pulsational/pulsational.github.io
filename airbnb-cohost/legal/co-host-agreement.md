## 1. SERVICES PROVIDED

Co-Host agrees to provide the following services for the Rental Property:

### 1.1 Listing Management
- Create and optimize Airbnb listing (photos, description, pricing)
- Manage listing on Airbnb platform
- Update calendar and availability
- Set and adjust pricing (dynamic pricing strategies)
- Monitor and respond to reviews

### 1.2 Guest Communication
- Respond to all guest inquiries within 1 hour during business hours (9 AM - 10 PM)
- Manage booking requests and reservations
- Send check-in/check-out instructions
- Provide 24/7 emergency response

### 1.3 Operations Management
- Coordinate cleaning between guest stays
- Conduct pre-arrival and post-departure inspections
- Manage check-in and check-out processes
- Coordinate maintenance and repairs (with Owner approval for costs >$[X])
- Manage supplies and restocking

### 1.4 Compliance & Administration
- Ensure compliance with City of San Jose short-term rental regulations
- Monitor 180-day annual cap (unhosted rentals)
- Assist with business license application (if needed)
- Verify TOT tax collection through Airbnb
- Maintain booking records

## 2. COMPENSATION

### 2.1 Co-Host Fee
Co-Host shall receive **[25]%** of the **Gross Rental Income** for each reservation.

**Gross Rental Income** is defined as:
- Nightly rate × number of nights
- Cleaning fees
- **Excluding:** Airbnb service fees, TOT taxes, refunds, cancellations

### 2.2 Payment Structure
- Airbnb payouts go directly to **Owner's account**
- Owner pays Co-Host within **[7] business days** after each guest checkout
- Payment method: [Zelle / Venmo / Bank transfer]

**Example Calculation:**
```
Guest booking: 5 nights × $180/night = $900
Cleaning fee: $100
Gross Rental Income: $1,000
Co-Host fee (25%): $250
Owner receives: $750
```

### 2.3 Expenses
**Owner is responsible for:**
- Cleaning costs (Co-Host coordinates, Owner pays directly)
- Supplies and restocking (toiletries, coffee, etc.)
- Utilities (electricity, water, gas, internet)
- Maintenance and repairs
- Property insurance
- Business license fee ($195/year in San Jose)
- HOA fees (if applicable)

**Co-Host is responsible for:**
- Co-Host's own business expenses (software, tools, phone)

## 3. TERM & TERMINATION

### 3.1 Initial Term
This Agreement begins on **[Start Date]** and continues for **[6/12] months** 
("Initial Term").

### 3.2 Renewal
This Agreement automatically renews for successive **[6/12]-month** periods 
unless terminated by either party.

### 3.3 Termination
Either party may terminate this Agreement:
- **With Cause:** Immediately, with written notice, if the other party breaches 
  this Agreement
- **Without Cause:** With **30 days' written notice**

### 3.4 Termination Obligations
Upon termination:
- Co-Host will transfer listing control back to Owner
- Co-Host will complete all confirmed reservations (or Owner may take over)
- Co-Host will provide all records and access credentials to Owner
- Outstanding fees shall be settled within 7 days

## 4. OWNER'S RESPONSIBILITIES

Owner agrees to:

- ✅ Provide Co-Host with full access to the Rental Property for inspections
- ✅ Maintain the property in good, rentable condition
- ✅ Obtain and maintain required business license (San Jose requires ~$195/year)
- ✅ Ensure property meets all safety requirements (smoke detectors, CO detector, etc.)
- ✅ Provide or install smart lock for self check-in
- ✅ Carry adequate property and liability insurance ($1M+ recommended)
- ✅ Comply with all San Jose short-term rental regulations, including:
  - Property must be Owner's primary residence (60+ days/year occupancy)
  - Comply with 180-day annual cap for unhosted rentals
  - Not rent out ADUs (prohibited in San Jose)
- ✅ Approve Co-Host's recommended pricing and house rules
- ✅ Respond to Co-Host's requests within 24 hours for maintenance decisions
- ✅ Pay Co-Host fees on time

## 5. CO-HOST'S RESPONSIBILITIES

Co-Host agrees to:

- ✅ Manage the Rental Property professionally and in Owner's best interest
- ✅ Respond to guest issues promptly (within 1 hour for emergencies)
- ✅ Maintain high guest satisfaction and Airbnb Superhost status (if applicable)
- ✅ Keep accurate records of all bookings, expenses, and communications
- ✅ Provide monthly financial reports to Owner
- ✅ Obtain Owner's approval before:
  - Making changes to listing that affect pricing by >20%
  - Incurring expenses >$[200] (emergency repairs exempt)
  - Accepting bookings that violate house rules or regulations
- ✅ Maintain confidentiality of Owner's personal information
- ✅ Not subcontract services without Owner's written consent

## 6. LIABILITY & INSURANCE

### 6.1 Owner's Insurance
Owner acknowledges that:
- Standard homeowner's insurance may NOT cover short-term rentals
- Owner should obtain appropriate commercial or short-term rental insurance
- Airbnb provides Host Protection Insurance (up to $3M), but it's secondary coverage

### 6.2 Co-Host Liability
- Co-Host is **not liable** for guest damages, injuries, or theft
- Co-Host will assist with Airbnb damage claims but is not financially responsible
- Owner agrees to indemnify and hold harmless Co-Host from any claims arising 
  from guest stays, except those caused by Co-Host's gross negligence or willful misconduct

### 6.3 Property Damage
- Co-Host will document property condition before/after each stay
- Co-Host will file Airbnb damage claims on Owner's behalf
- Owner is responsible for any unrecovered costs

---

## 7. COMPLIANCE & LEGAL

### 7.1 San Jose Regulations
Both parties acknowledge that short-term rentals in San Jose require:
- ✅ Property must be Owner's **primary residence**
- ✅ Business tax certificate (~$195/year)
- ✅ 10% Transient Occupancy Tax (TOT) — Airbnb auto-collects
- ✅ Maximum 180 nights/year for **unhosted** rentals
- ✅ Designated local contact (Co-Host serves this role)
- ✅ No ADUs allowed for short-term rental

**Owner represents and warrants** that the Property complies with all applicable 
laws and regulations.

### 7.2 Changes in Law
If new regulations prohibit or significantly restrict short-term rentals:
- Either party may terminate this Agreement with 30 days' notice
- No penalty for early termination due to regulatory changes

### 7.3 Violations & Fines
- Owner is responsible for any fines or penalties arising from non-compliance
- Co-Host will notify Owner immediately of any compliance issues
- Co-Host will monitor 180-day cap and block calendar when approaching limit
